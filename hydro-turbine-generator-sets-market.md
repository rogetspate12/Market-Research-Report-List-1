<p><ul><li>Francis Turbine</li><li>Kaplan Turbine</li><li>Diagonal Turbine</li><li>Tubular Turbine</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The hydro turbine generator sets market comprises various types of turbines, each suited for specific water flow conditions. Francis turbines operate efficiently with varying heads and are widely used in medium to large-scale plants. Kaplan turbines excel in low-head, high-flow applications, making them ideal for river-based projects. Diagonal turbines combine features of Francis and Kaplan, serving medium heads with higher flow rates. Tubular turbines are designed for high flow, low-head scenarios, commonly applied in irrigation and run-of-the-river systems.</p></p>

<p><ul><li>Impulse Turbines</li><li>Reaction Turbines</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The hydro turbine generator sets market encompasses two main types: impulse and reaction turbines. Impulse turbines, such as Pelton wheels, convert the kinetic energy of water jets into mechanical energy, ideal for high-head applications. Reaction turbines, like Francis and Kaplan turbines, utilize both kinetic and pressure energy, suitable for medium to low-head situations. These technologies are crucial for harnessing renewable energy from water sources, powering grids, and supporting sustainable development in various regions.</p></p>
