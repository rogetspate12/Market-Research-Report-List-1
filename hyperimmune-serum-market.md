<p><p>The hyperimmune serum market is categorized into several types based on the specific immunoglobulins they contain. Hepatitis B immunoglobulins provide passive immunity against hepatitis B virus. Rabies immunoglobulins are crucial for post-exposure prophylaxis, preventing rabies after potential exposure. Tetanus immunoglobulins offer protection against tetanus, especially in unvaccinated individuals. Rho(D) immunoglobulins are used to prevent Rh incompatibility in pregnant women. Other markets may include immunoglobulins for infections like cytomegalovirus or varicella, enhancing immunity against various diseases.</p></p>
