<p><p>Polymer materials used for drug packaging include several key types, each with unique properties. PVC (polyvinyl chloride) is known for its durability and versatility, commonly used in blister packs and containers. PE (polyethylene) offers excellent moisture resistance, suitable for flexible packaging. PET (polyethylene terephthalate) provides clarity and strength, often utilized for bottles and rigid containers. PP (polypropylene) offers high-temperature resistance and is ideal for sterilized packaging. Other polymers may include PS (polystyrene) and EVOH (ethylene vinyl alcohol), catering to specific packaging needs in the pharmaceutical industry.</p></p>
